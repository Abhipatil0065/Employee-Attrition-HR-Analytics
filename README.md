HR Analytics Dashboard — Power BI
Business Problem
Organizations with large workforces often struggle to identify why employees leave and which departments or age groups are most affected by attrition. 
Without a centralized view, HR teams make decisions based on incomplete data, leading to high recruitment costs and loss of institutional knowledge. 
This project addresses the challenge of understanding employee attrition patterns across departments, age bands, education backgrounds, and job roles.


Goal of the Dashboard
The goal was to build an interactive HR analytics dashboard that gives HR managers and business leaders a single view of workforce health — tracking overall headcount, attrition rate, active employees, and satisfaction levels.
The dashboard enables data-driven decisions around retention strategies and workforce planning.


Key Visuals Used
The dashboard includes KPI cards showing overall employees (1,470), attrition count (237), attrition rate (16%), active employees (1,233), and average employee age (36.92). 
A pie chart breaks down attrition by department — Sales contributing the highest at 56.12%, followed by R&D at 38.82% and HR at 5.06%. 
A stacked bar chart shows attrition distribution across age bands (Under 25 to Over 55) split by gender. 
Four donut charts highlight attrition counts for specific age groups individually.
A horizontal bar chart shows attrition by education field, with Life Sciences leading. 
A job satisfaction matrix table shows satisfaction scores (1 to 4) across all job roles.



Business Impact
This dashboard helps HR leadership quickly identify high-risk departments and age segments for attrition, allowing them to prioritize retention programs where they matter most.
It reduces the time spent on manual HR reporting and gives leadership actionable data to reduce hiring costs associated with frequent employee turnover.



Key Insights Derived
Sales department has the highest attrition at 56%, making it the most critical area for retention focus. 
Employees in the 25 to 34 age band show the highest attrition volume, suggesting early-career dissatisfaction or better external opportunities.
Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds account for the majority of attrition in terms of education field.
Among job roles, Research Scientists and Laboratory Technicians show the largest headcount in satisfaction level 3 and 4, indicating moderate-to-high satisfaction, while Human Resources roles have the lowest overall satisfaction totals.
The overall 16% attrition rate signals a need for structured engagement and compensation review programs.
